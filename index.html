Hello, friends. I’m glad you are reading this. I have done so much research t o bring me to this page. I am sad to say that nearly 100% of bird threats involv e human activity. Agriculture alone puts 74% of the bird species in the entire w orld at risk of extinction. So I want to emphasize something many people don’t u nderstand, and I want you to be here to hear it: It isn’t fair how birds are tre ated. I realize that many people think of birds as just an animal, and animals s eem to be invisible to human activity. It’s like we don’t care that we harm the animals. So many people don’t know how bad this is to our environment and ecosys tem. But I believe it is important to value every living being as a true living being. I did focus this book on birds, but they aren’t the only ones suffering. I hope you join me in saving wildlife, for many of these natural wonders were he re long before we were. They respect us. Why don’t we respect them? Well, we can . But we don’t.

After reading my articles, I hope as you look out through your window and wat ch these birds hop around, you realize that they cause no harm. It is us doing a ll the damage, from tearing down their houses to polluting the air to using harm ful chemicals. They are neighbors to us and are part of the planet Earth family. We seem like it’s no big deal, but it is. We harmed their peaceful lives, so we will fix it. It is our job to preserve birds. There are many ways to help, and I will be talking about my experience with birds and how I helped.

I started loving birds when I began writing this book, which is why I chose t his as the topic. A bit after that, my mom bought me a hummingbird feeder and we hung it right outside the kitchen. It became a regular thing for Broad-Tailed H ummingbirds and Anna’s Hummingbirds to visit everyday. We also planted flowers t hat attracted these little hummingbirds. If you are interested, you can get a fe eder, or maybe some flowers (red stands out to hummingbirds). Make sure feeders have fresh sugar water so that the hummingbirds will drink it. Sugar water is si mple to make: just boil a pot of water and stir in sugar. Your yard can be a saf e habitat for hummingbirds if pesticides aren’t used and there isn’t too much gl ass. There should also be shrubbery and trees for shelter and for the place to l ook and feel more like their natural habitats. I know at least one hummingbird h as been happy living near my backyard.

Later, after about 1 or 2 years, my dad got me a bird feeder when my passion for birds sank even deeper. The camera allowed me to see the bird up close and c learly, something we can’t do with bare human eyes. I loved watching songbirds c ome through the app. It was a great opportunity for me to watch new species eat and enjoy the food. For example, the Chestnut-Backed Chickadee and House Finch w ere two new species I had never seen in the backyard. Feeders like this one help you more closely admire the beauty of birds of all shapes and sizes. Though it has not been done yet, my mom and I are planning to add a birdhouse to our backy ard, another cozy safe addition to our bird-friendly backyard.

Hawks may be occasional visitors to feeders. Songbirds are attracted to areas with easy food, and so are hawks. If a hawk or eagle finds its way to your feed er and tries to kill the little feeder visitors, it is best to remove the feeder for a few days so it attracts less birds and the hawk should move on. Remember that hawks are also part of the ecosystem, so we shouldn’t hurt or cage them.

Even though none were written about in these chapters, endangered birds still exist everywhere. Around 90 are found in only the USA! Ones in the Bay Area inc lude the California Chapper Rail, the California Least Tern, the Western Snowy P lover, the Marbled Murrlet, and the Burrowing Owl. We can also see that preserva tion in action is helpful. As written in the chapter about them, Peregrine Falco ns used to be endangered, but thanks to conservation efforts, they have bounced back.

Currently, we are doing the opposite and hurting our feathered friends. We ar e expanding cities, not caring about the animals we hurt along the way. Birds ar e being forced to live in urban places, so we can create a safe home right near our house and give the birds some habitats. We can make cities bird-friendly eco systems and continue to explore the wonder and beauty of birds! I encourage you to do your own research to explore the many ways to help keep the partners of ou r planet alive. Even going for 1 species and making a difference is progress. On e person can’t save every bird. But the more people cooperating, the more birds can be helped. Step one is to learn. I want this website to help people understa nd birds so that they can help. I made it so that people can have an adventure i nto one of nature’s miraculous creations: birds. All you have to do now is to do it. Take action in saving them. Teach others to value birds as true living bein gs. It’s as simple as donating to a bird friendly organization. Or turning your yard into a bird welcome ecosystem. Start today, start now. You decide when. I c an only provide information and encouragement. But I hope you work with me—and m any others—to help birds. And in the future, respect them. Like we should.
